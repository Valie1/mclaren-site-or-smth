Chapter 01 — Le Mans Legend
Born to
Devour Roads
The McLaren F1 GT wasn't built for racetracks — it was built to win Le Mans. Three works-specification GT cars were prepared for the 1995 24 Hours of Le Mans, and the F1 GTR claimed victory outright on its debut. The road-going GT version carries that DNA to every public road it graces.

Chapter 02 — Gordon Murray's Vision
Carbon
& Gold
Designed by Gordon Murray, the F1 GT features a full carbon fibre monocoque chassis — the first road car to do so. The engine bay is lined with gold foil to reflect heat. Every gram was scrutinised, every surface had a purpose. Only five GT variants were ever produced, each one a masterwork of 1990s engineering philosophy.

Chapter 03 — How This Experience Was Built
Behind the
Scene
This cinematic tribute to the McLaren F1 GT is built entirely in the browser — no plugins, no pre-rendered video. Every frame is calculated in real time using WebGL and Three.js, with physically-based rendering, dynamic lighting, and shader-driven atmospherics.
01 — 3D RENDERING
Three.js + WebGL
The McLaren F1 GT and tunnel models are loaded as GLB files using Three.js's GLTFLoader. Physically Based Rendering (PBR) materials respond to the scene's dynamic lighting in real time, giving the bodywork that deep, automotive sheen.
THREE.JS r128 GLTF 2.0 PBR
02 — LIGHTING RIG
Cinematic Lighting
A custom lighting rig combines directional key lights, orange-tinted fill lights, a deep blue rim light for separation, and animated point lights that travel through the tunnel — mimicking the practical lighting of a real automotive shoot.
DIRECTIONAL LIGHTS POINT LIGHTS ENVIRONMENT MAP
03 — CAMERA SYSTEM
Cinematic Camera
The camera follows a bezier spline path through the tunnel, shifting between a rear-tracking shot, dramatic low-angle side views, and a front-chasing perspective. Smooth easing and FOV changes simulate real camera motion.
BEZIER SPLINES FOV SHIFTS EASING CURVES
04 — VISUAL FX
Post-Processing & FX
CSS scanlines, a radial vignette, speed-line canvas overlays, and a custom HUD are layered over the WebGL canvas. The cinematic letterbox bars open on load. A custom cursor, glare flash, and counter animation add to the immersion.
CANVAS 2D CSS ANIMATION SHADER-LIKE FX
Chapter 04 — The Legacy
The Last
True F1
When the McLaren F1 was unveiled in 1992, nothing could touch it. When the GT variant emerged in 1997, it had already proven itself at Le Mans. Today, it remains one of the most visceral, uncompromised driving machines ever conceived — a car that needed no driver aids, no turbos, no compromise. Just the road, the engine, and you.
